How Much Does a Website Cost in South Africa? (2026 Guide)

April 2026 · By Elevate Digitals · 5 min read

Website prices in South Africa vary wildly — from R2,000 for a DIY template to R200,000+ for a custom enterprise solution. If you've tried Googling this, you've probably been left more confused than when you started. This guide cuts through the noise and tells you exactly what you're getting at each price point.

What Actually Affects the Price?

  • Design complexity — A custom design costs more than a template, but it uniquely represents your brand and converts better.
  • Number of pages — More pages = more time. A 10-page site takes roughly 3x longer than a 3-page site.
  • Features — E-commerce, booking systems, payment gateways, and CMS all add development time and cost.
  • SEO requirements — Basic on-page SEO is usually included. Comprehensive keyword research and monthly reporting costs more.
  • Content creation — Professional copywriting and photography add to the total but significantly improve results.
  • Ongoing maintenance — Security updates, backups, and content changes typically cost R499–R1,999/month.

Why Cheaper Isn't Always Better

A R2,000 DIY website might seem like a bargain, but consider this: if your website loads slowly, looks unprofessional, or can't be found on Google, it's actively costing you customers. A poorly built website can hurt your business more than having no website at all.

Studies show that 53% of mobile visitors leave a site that takes longer than 3 seconds to load. Speed, design, and SEO are investments, not luxuries.
