How to Use WhatsApp to Get More Customers From Your Website

April 2026 · By Elevate Digitals · 4 min read

WhatsApp is used by over 90% of South African smartphone users. It's the communication channel your customers are most comfortable with — and adding it to your website is one of the simplest, highest-impact things you can do to increase enquiries and sales.

3–5x Businesses that add a WhatsApp chat button to their website typically see 3–5x more customer enquiries compared to contact forms alone.

Why WhatsApp Works So Well in South Africa

  • It's familiar — Most South Africans check WhatsApp dozens of times a day. A WhatsApp message feels natural, not formal.
  • Lower barrier to entry — Filling out a contact form feels like commitment. Sending a WhatsApp message feels like a quick chat.
  • Instant responses build trust — Real-time conversation converts faster than back-and-forth emails.
  • Works on mobile — Since 80%+ of South African users browse on phones, a tap-to-WhatsApp link is frictionless.
  • Pre-filled messages — You can link to WhatsApp with a pre-written message like "Hi, I'm interested in your services" — removing all effort from the customer.

Where to Add WhatsApp on Your Website

Placement is everything. Here's where WhatsApp buttons perform best:

  • Floating sticky button — A WhatsApp icon that floats in the bottom right corner of every page. This is the highest-converting placement.
  • Hero section — Next to your main call-to-action on the homepage. "Chat with us on WhatsApp" alongside "Get a Quote".
  • Pricing pages — When visitors are weighing up options, a "Questions? Chat with us" link closes the gap.
  • Contact page — As a supplement to your contact form, not a replacement.
  • Blog posts — At the end of articles, offering to answer any questions directly.

How to Set Up WhatsApp Links

WhatsApp click-to-chat links use this format:

https://wa.me/[your-number]?text=[pre-filled message]

Example: https://wa.me/27650858437?text=Hi%2C%20I%27m%20interested%20in%20a%20website

Replace the number with your full international format (27 + number without the leading 0).

Best Practices

  • Use a business WhatsApp account — WhatsApp Business gives you a profile, opening hours, and quick replies.
  • Set up auto-replies — An away message like "Thanks for reaching out! We'll reply within 2 hours during business hours" manages expectations.
  • Use green branding — The WhatsApp green (#25D366) is universally recognised. Don't change the colour.
  • Don't make it your only contact option — Some customers prefer email or forms. Offer both.
  • Respond fast — The biggest mistake is having WhatsApp on your site but taking 24 hours to reply. It kills trust instantly.
